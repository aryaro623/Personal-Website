ABOUT_ME

I build systems with enough utility to matter and enough personality to actually use.

I’m a finance and economics student who uses AI to build around the workflows I actually live: money, discipline, work, learning, trading, and decision-making. My projects usually start as a raw question, frustration, or self-audit, then turn into dashboards, automations, assistants, or operating systems.

The pattern is simple: notice friction, structure the problem, build the smallest useful system, test it against real behavior, then refine until it becomes something durable. I care about tools that help people act with more clarity, not demos that only look impressive once.
